N (Nitrogen content in soil)
Represents the amount of Nitrogen present in the soil (in kg/ha).
Nitrogen is essential for plant growth, especially for leaf development.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
P (Phosphorus content in soil)
Amount of Phosphorus present in the soil (in kg/ha).
Helps in root development and flower/fruit production.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
K (Potassium content in soil)
Amount of Potassium present in the soil (in kg/ha).
Important for disease resistance, water regulation, and crop quality.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
temperature
The average temperature of the region (in °C).
Crops require different temperature ranges to grow optimally.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
humidity
Relative humidity of the air (in %).
Higher humidity helps some crops (like rice), while others prefer dry conditions.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ph
Soil pH value (0–14 scale, usually 4–9 in agriculture).
Indicates acidity or alkalinity of soil; most crops prefer slightly acidic to neutral soil (6–7.5).
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
rainfall
Average rainfall (in mm).
Different crops need different water levels; e.g., rice requires heavy rainfall, whereas wheat needs moderate water.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
label
The target variable (crop name).
Example: rice, maize, chickpea, banana, etc.
This is what we predict using machine learning based on soil and climate conditions.
